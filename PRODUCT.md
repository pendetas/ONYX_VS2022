# Product

## Register

product

## Users

ONYX serves customers shopping for gaming peripherals and account owners returning for cart, wishlist, order history, warranty, reviews, and support. Users are usually comparing gear quickly, checking ownership details, or trying to resolve a purchase/support question without getting lost.

## Product Purpose

ONYX is a B2C gaming gear ecommerce site for mice, keyboards, headsets, monitors, and accessories. The product experience should help customers browse, compare, save, buy, review, and get support for ONYX gear with confidence. Success means the storefront, account tools, and support paths feel like one coherent ownership journey.

## Brand Personality

Restrained, technical, confident. ONYX should feel like black-and-silver performance hardware: precise, calm under pressure, and practical rather than noisy or gimmicky.

## Anti-references

Avoid loud gamer neon overload, generic SaaS cards, decorative gradients, oversized rounded panels, unclear ecommerce actions, and support experiences that repeat the same warranty claims instead of telling customers what to do next.

## Design Principles

1. Keep ownership paths clear: catalog, wishlist, cart, orders, reviews, and support should connect around the customer account.
2. Make product differences practical: focus on control, response, sound, comfort, setup fit, stock, and warranty context.
3. Preserve the black-and-silver system: restrained dark surfaces, high contrast, sharp edges, and purposeful green/white state cues.
4. Prefer familiar ecommerce affordances: buttons, chips, forms, and panels should feel instantly usable before they feel expressive.
5. Reduce interruption: help tools should guide users without blocking payment, login, registration, or checkout flows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target readable contrast, visible focus states, keyboard-operable controls, mobile touch targets of at least 44px, reduced-motion support, and copy that explains recovery paths when API or support flows fail.
